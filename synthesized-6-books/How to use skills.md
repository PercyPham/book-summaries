## The chaining logic

The three skills map to the three moments in a launch cycle: **decide → produce → verify**. Used well, they form a loop, not three separate tools:

```
business-growth-advisor  →  direct-response-copywriter  →  offer-funnel-auditor
   ("what should I do?")      ("write it")                    ("is it good enough to ship?")
        ↑_____________________________________________________________|
                    (audit reveals a strategic gap → back to advisor)
```

You don't have to invoke them by name — the descriptions trigger automatically based on what you ask for. But naming one explicitly ("use the auditor on this") helps when a request could plausibly go two ways.

## Worked example — hypothetical: online cooking course, mediocre sales

**Step 1 — Diagnose (advisor).**

> "I sell a $200 cooking course. I get traffic but barely any sales. What should I fix?"

It diagnoses the constraint (say: no capture/nurture — cold traffic hits the sales page directly) and recommends the _one_ next move: build a lead magnet + opt-in before touching traffic. This is the same behavior you saw in the earlier test run.

**Step 2 — Produce (copywriter).**

> "Write me a lead magnet title and opt-in page for that recommendation — dream buyer is home cooks intimidated by technique."

It drafts a curiosity-driven HVCO title, fascination bullets, and the opt-in page skeleton — ready to paste, with 2–3 headline variants.

**Step 3 — Verify (auditor).**

> "Audit this opt-in page before I publish it: [paste the draft]"

It grades every element (headline, bullets, bait strength, form friction, CTA) with ✅/⚠️/❌ and gives an overall verdict — _ship it_, _fix first_, or _rebuild_, plus a prioritized punch list.

**Step 4 — Close the loop.**
If the auditor flags something (e.g., "bullets are feature-y, not curiosity-teasers ⚠️"), go back to the copywriter with that exact fix — it's already a concrete, actionable note, so the rewrite is fast. Re-audit. Once it scores clean, ship it.

**Step 5 — Zoom back out (advisor).**
A few weeks later: _"Opt-in page is live, converting at 4%. What's next?"_ — the advisor re-diagnoses using fresh numbers and gives you the next link in the chain (traffic, nurture sequence, etc.), and the loop repeats at the next asset.

## A few things that make this work well

- **Give the auditor the real asset, not a description.** It's explicitly built to refuse grading something it can't see — paste the copy or a link.
- **Give the copywriter the inputs it needs up front** (customer, price, traffic temperature) or let it ask — vague briefs produce vague copy.
- **Let the advisor push back.** If it says the real problem is upstream of what you asked about, that's it doing its job — don't route around it to the copywriter just to get the asset you originally wanted.
- **You can chain in one message** too: _"Write me an ad hook for X, then audit it"_ — both skills can fire in the same turn.
- **Works identically across chat, Cowork, and Claude Code** — same SKILL.md, same behavior, since you installed the same files in each.
